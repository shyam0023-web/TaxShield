# Section 74 — Determination of Tax in Fraud Cases

## Key Ruling
Section 74 applies to cases involving **fraud, willful misstatement, or suppression of facts** with intent to evade tax. Limitation period is **5 years** from the due date of filing annual return (GSTR-9).

## Limitation Period
- **Time limit:** 5 years from due date of annual return
- **Example:** FY 2017-18 → GSTR-9 due 31-Dec-2018 → SCN must be issued by **31-Dec-2023**

## COVID Extensions
- FY 2017-18: Extended to 31-Mar-2026 (Notification 09/2023)
- FY 2018-19: Extended to 31-Mar-2027 (Notification 09/2023)

## Penalty Under Section 74
- **Mandatory penalty** of 100% of tax amount (equal to tax demanded)
- If taxpayer pays tax + interest + 15% penalty within 30 days of SCN → proceedings dropped (Section 74(5))
- If paid within 30 days of order → penalty reduced to 25% (Section 74(11))

## Burden of Proof
Under Section 74, the **burden is on the department** to prove:
1. The existence of fraud/willful misstatement/suppression
2. Intent to evade tax
3. If department fails to establish intent, Section 73 applies instead (3-year limit)

## Key Defense Points
1. Challenge "fraud/suppression" invocation — demand department prove intent
2. If facts were disclosed in GSTR-1/3B, suppression cannot be alleged
3. If extended limitation period used without establishing fraud → **notice invalid**
4. Section 74 demands attract higher penalty — verify if department met burden of proof
5. Pre-SCN intimation (DRC-01A) is mandatory even under Section 74

## Relevant Circulars
- CGST Circular 42/2018: Extended limitation period for fraud cases
