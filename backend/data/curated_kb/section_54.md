# Section 54 — Refund of Tax

## Summary
Section 54 governs the refund mechanism under GST for excess tax paid, accumulated ITC on zero-rated supplies, inverted duty structure, and other situations.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 54(1):** Any person claiming refund must apply within 2 years from the relevant date.
- **Section 54(2):** Refund on zero-rated supplies — exporter can claim refund of IGST paid or accumulated ITC.
- **Section 54(3):** Refund of accumulated ITC allowed in two cases:
  - (a) Zero-rated supplies without payment of tax
  - (b) Inverted duty structure (input tax rate > output tax rate)
- **Section 54(5):** If refund is not sanctioned within 60 days, interest at 6% per annum is payable.
- **Section 54(8):** Unjust enrichment check — refund to consumer welfare fund if burden passed to buyer.
- **Section 54(14):** No refund on goods notified by Government on recommendation of GST Council.

## Key Ruling
In **VKC Footsteps India Pvt Ltd v. Union of India**, the Supreme Court held that refund under inverted duty structure must be calculated as per Rule 89(5) which restricts refund to input goods only (not input services). However, CBIC Circular 135/2020 clarified that ITC on input services is included in net ITC for the formula.

## Key Defense Points
1. Two-year limitation period runs from the "relevant date" defined in Explanation to Section 54 — calculate carefully
2. For exporters, refund cannot be denied if IGST was paid and export is substantiated by shipping bill
3. Interest at 6% is automatic if refund exceeds 60 days — file formal interest claim
4. Unjust enrichment does not apply to exporters and to cases where tax was borne by the applicant
5. Deficiency memo under Rule 89(3) must be issued within 15 days — delay means deemed acceptance
6. CBIC Circular 125/2019 and 135/2020 are binding on officers regarding refund calculations
