# DIN Requirement — Circular 128/47/2019

## Key Ruling
Every communication issued by GST officers (SCNs, orders, summons, letters) **MUST bear a Document Identification Number (DIN)**. Any communication without a DIN is **deemed to have never been issued** and is treated as **invalid and non-est (non-existent in law)**.

## Legal Basis
- CBIC Circular No. 128/47/2019-GST dated 23-Dec-2019
- CBIC Circular No. 122/41/2019-GST (initial circular on DIN)
- Board's Instruction F.No. 390/Misc./116/2017-JC

## Exceptions (Communications exempt from DIN)
1. Oral communications during search/seizure operations
2. Communications issued on CBIC/GSTN portal directly
3. Summons issued manually during ongoing search

## Key Defense Points
1. **No DIN = invalid notice** — this is the strongest procedural defense available
2. If notice/order does not mention DIN → demand its production
3. If DIN was generated AFTER the notice was issued → notice still invalid (must be contemporaneous)
4. Even if entire notice is substantively correct, absence of DIN renders it void
5. Multiple High Courts have upheld DIN requirement as mandatory, not directory

## How to Verify DIN
- Check the DIN on CBIC's DIN verification portal: https://appdcbic.gov.in
- DIN format: typically alphanumeric, mentioned at top or bottom of communication
