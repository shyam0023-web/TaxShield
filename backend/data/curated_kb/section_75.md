# Section 75 — General Provisions for Demand Proceedings

## Key Ruling
Section 75 lays down the **procedural requirements** that must be followed in every demand proceeding under Sections 73, 74, 76, and 122-125. Non-compliance with these provisions can render an order **voidable**.

## Mandatory Requirements
1. **Section 75(4) — Opportunity of Personal Hearing:** Before passing any adverse order, the proper officer MUST offer an **opportunity of personal hearing** if:
   - Requested by the noticee, OR
   - Any adverse decision is contemplated
2. **Section 75(5) — Written Order with Reasons:** Every order must be a **reasoned order in writing** setting out relevant facts, applicable law, and basis of determination
3. **Section 75(6) — Time Limit for Order:**
   - Under Section 73: Order within **3 years** from SCN date
   - Under Section 74: Order within **5 years** from SCN date
4. **Section 75(7) — Amount Determination:** Demand amount must be computed with reference to documents and evidence on record

## Key Defense Points
1. If personal hearing not offered/granted → **order violates Section 75(4), liable to be set aside**
2. If order does not contain detailed reasoning → **non-speaking order, bad in law**
3. If order passed beyond time limit → **void, time-barred**
4. If demand amount calculated without detailed working → **challenge under Section 75(7)**
5. Every adjournment request must be considered — blanket rejection violates natural justice

## Relevant Principles
- **Audi alteram partem** (hear the other side) — fundamental principle of natural justice
- **Quasi-judicial proceedings** — GST officers act as quasi-judicial authorities
- **Doctrine of proportionality** — penalty must be proportionate to the violation
