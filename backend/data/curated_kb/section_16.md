# Section 16 — Eligibility and Conditions for Taking Input Tax Credit

## Summary
Section 16 of the CGST Act, 2017 lays down the eligibility and conditions for claiming Input Tax Credit (ITC). It is one of the most frequently litigated sections in GST law.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 16(1):** Every registered person shall be entitled to take credit of input tax charged on any supply of goods or services.
- **Section 16(2):** Conditions for ITC:
  - (a) Possession of tax invoice or debit note
  - (b) Receipt of goods or services
  - (c) Tax actually paid to the government
  - (d) Furnishing of return under Section 39
- **Section 16(3):** If depreciation is claimed on tax component, ITC not allowed.
- **Section 16(4):** Time limit — ITC must be claimed before the due date of filing return for September of the following year, or the date of filing annual return, whichever is earlier.

## Key Ruling
The Supreme Court in **Union of India v. Bharti Airtel Ltd.** held that ITC is a vested right and cannot be denied on procedural grounds if substantive conditions are met. Section 16(2) conditions are mandatory but must be read harmoniously with the right to ITC under Section 16(1).

## Key Defense Points
1. ITC is a substantive right under GST law and cannot be denied on mere technicalities
2. If GSTR-2A/2B shows the supplier has paid tax, ITC cannot be denied to the recipient
3. Section 16(4) time limit can be challenged if there were genuine reasons for delay
4. Rule 36(4) restriction (limiting ITC to 110% of GSTR-2A) has been struck down by several High Courts
5. Non-filing of return by supplier cannot result in denial of ITC to a bona fide recipient
6. CBIC Circular 183/15/2022 clarifies that ITC mismatch alone is not sufficient ground for denial

## Common SCN Issues
- GSTR-2A vs GSTR-3B mismatch
- ITC claimed on ineligible supplies (Section 17(5))
- ITC reversal for non-payment within 180 days
- ITC claimed beyond the time limit under Section 16(4)
