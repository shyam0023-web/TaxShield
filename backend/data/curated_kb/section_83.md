# Section 83 — Provisional Attachment to Protect Revenue

## Summary
Section 83 empowers the Commissioner to provisionally attach property of a taxable person during pendency of proceedings under Sections 62, 63, 64, 67, 73, or 74 to protect the interest of revenue.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 83(1):** Commissioner may attach any property including bank accounts during pendency of specified proceedings.
- **Section 83(2):** Provisional attachment shall cease after expiry of 1 year from the date of the order.
- **Rule 159:** Procedure for provisional attachment — order in Form GST DRC-22, objection in DRC-22A.

## Key Ruling
In **Radha Krishan Industries v. State of Himachal Pradesh**, the Supreme Court laid down the following principles:
1. Provisional attachment is a drastic power and must be exercised with extreme care
2. There must be a proximate and live nexus between the proceedings and the attachment
3. Attachment of the entire business or all bank accounts is disproportionate
4. The Commissioner must form an opinion that attachment is necessary to protect revenue — mere initiation of proceedings is not sufficient

## Key Defense Points
1. Attachment automatically lapses after 1 year — no renewal is permitted under law
2. Only the Commissioner (not lower officers) can order attachment — delegation is invalid
3. Attachment must be proportionate to the probable tax demand — cannot freeze all bank accounts
4. Revenue must demonstrate that the taxpayer intends to dispose of assets to evade tax
5. Objection under Rule 159(5) in Form DRC-22A must be decided within 7 working days
6. Attachment of personal property of directors/partners is not permitted unless they are personally liable
7. Writ petition challenging disproportionate attachment is maintainable and courts frequently grant relief
