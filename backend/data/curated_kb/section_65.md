# Section 65 — Audit by Tax Authorities

## Key Ruling
Section 65 empowers the Commissioner to undertake **audit** of any registered person for a financial year. Audit period cannot cover more than **5 financial years** at a time.

## Audit Process
1. Commissioner issues audit notice with minimum **15 working days** advance notice
2. Audit conducted at taxpayer's place of business or at the tax office
3. Audit must be completed within **3 months** from commencement (extendable by 6 months)
4. Audit findings communicated in **Form ADT-02** within 30 days of completion
5. If discrepancies found → may lead to proceedings under Section 73 or 74

## Key Defense Points
1. If audit notice does not give 15 working days → **procedural defect**
2. If audit exceeds 3+6 = 9 months without extension → **time limit breached**
3. Audit observations are NOT demands — they are findings that must lead to proper SCN
4. Cooperate with audit but object to overbroad document requests
5. If audit findings lead to Section 73/74 → all normal defenses apply
6. ADT-02 must specify exact discrepancies — vague observations are challengeable

## Documents Typically Requested
- Purchase and sales registers
- GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9 for audit period
- E-way bills
- Bank statements
- Stock registers
- Input tax credit register
