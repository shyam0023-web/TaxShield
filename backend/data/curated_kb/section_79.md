# Section 79 — Recovery of Tax

## Summary
Section 79 provides the mechanisms available to the government for recovering tax dues from a defaulting taxpayer. This is the section invoked after an adjudication order confirms the demand.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 79(1):** Recovery methods available to proper officer:
  - (a) Deduction from any amount payable to the defaulter by the government
  - (b) Recovery by detaining and selling goods belonging to the defaulter
  - (c) Recovery from third parties who owe money to the defaulter (garnishee notice)
  - (d) Distrain of movable/immovable property
  - (e) Through Collector of district (revenue recovery)
  - (f) Through Magistrate (as if it were a fine imposed by Magistrate)
- **Section 79(2):** Recovery from surety or any person who has guaranteed.
- **Section 79(3):** If any amount is stayed by an Appellate Authority/Tribunal/Court, recovery shall be deemed stayed.

## Key Ruling
In **Radha Krishan Industries v. State of Himachal Pradesh**, the Supreme Court held that coercive recovery methods should not be adopted if the demand itself is under challenge. The officer must exercise restraint while the matter is sub judice.

## Key Defense Points
1. Recovery is automatically stayed once pre-deposit is made under Section 107(6) appeal
2. Garnishee notice to bank/debtor under Section 79(1)(c) is invalid if the demand itself is disputed
3. Recovery cannot be initiated during the 3-month appeal period after adjudication order
4. Writ petition can be filed challenging coercive recovery if appeal remedies are being exhausted
5. Provisional attachment under Section 83 expires after 1 year and cannot be renewed indefinitely
6. Recovery proceedings must follow due process — attachment of personal property without notice is illegal
