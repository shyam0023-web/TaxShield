# Section 30 — Revocation of Cancellation of Registration

## Summary
Section 30 allows a registered person whose registration has been cancelled under Section 29 to apply for revocation within 30 days of the cancellation order.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 30(1):** Application for revocation must be filed within 30 days of service of cancellation order.
- **Section 30(2):** Proper officer may revoke cancellation if satisfied that grounds no longer exist.
- **Section 30(3):** Proviso — all pending returns must be filed and all dues must be paid before revocation.

## Key Ruling
Multiple High Courts including Madras, Allahabad, and Delhi have held that the 30-day time limit is procedural and can be condoned if the taxpayer shows sufficient cause. The right to carry on business cannot be curtailed by rigid adherence to procedural time limits.

## Key Defense Points
1. The 30-day limit has been extended to 90 days by Additional/Joint Commissioner and 180 days by Commissioner per CBIC notifications
2. Filing all pending returns and paying dues before applying strengthens the case for revocation
3. Retrospective cancellation should be revoked if the taxpayer was actively conducting business during the period
4. Multiple amnesty schemes (2023, 2024) have allowed revocation even beyond the normal time limit
5. Cancellation for non-filing can be revoked by simply filing all pending returns with late fees
