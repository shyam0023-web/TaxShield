# Section 67 — Inspection, Search, and Seizure

## Key Ruling
Section 67 empowers a Joint Commissioner (or above) to authorize **inspection** of any place of business if there are **reasons to believe** that goods or documents relevant to GST proceedings are concealed.

## Process
1. **Inspection (67(1)):** Authorized officer can inspect any place of business
2. **Search (67(2)):** If after inspection, officer has reasons to believe goods/documents are concealed → may authorize **search and seizure**
3. Seized goods must be returned within **6 months** (extendable by 6 months by Commissioner)
4. Provisional release of seized goods possible under **Section 67(6)** on furnishing bond + security

## Key Defense Points
1. Authorization must be by **Joint Commissioner or above** — if authorized by lower rank → **illegal search**
2. Authorization must record **reasons to believe** — not reasons to suspect
3. If seized goods not returned within 6+6 = 12 months → **must be released**
4. Search must be conducted during business hours unless urgency documented
5. Panchnama (seizure memo) is mandatory — challenge if not prepared
6. Statement recorded under Section 67(10) can be retracted if taken under coercion
