# Section 17 — Apportionment of Credit and Blocked Credits

## Summary
Section 17 of the CGST Act deals with apportionment of ITC when goods/services are used partly for business and partly for other purposes, and lists blocked credits under Section 17(5).

## Key Provisions
- **Section 17(1):** ITC available only for goods/services used in the course or furtherance of business.
- **Section 17(2):** If used partly for business and partly for non-business, ITC allowed only for the business portion.
- **Section 17(3):** ITC for taxable and exempt supplies — proportionate reversal required (Rule 42 & 43).
- **Section 17(5):** Blocked credits — ITC NOT available for:
  - (a) Motor vehicles (except specific cases)
  - (b) Food and beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services
  - (c) Membership of club, health and fitness centre
  - (d) Rent-a-cab, life/health insurance (except for specified purposes)
  - (e) Travel benefits to employees on vacation
  - (f) Works contract services for construction of immovable property
  - (g) Goods/services for construction of immovable property on own account
  - (h) Tax paid under composition scheme
  - (i) Goods/services received by non-resident taxable person
  - (j) Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, or given as free samples/gifts

## Key Ruling
In **Safari Retreats Pvt Ltd v. Chief Commissioner CGST**, the Supreme Court held that ITC on construction of immovable property used for taxable supply (renting) is NOT blocked under Section 17(5)(d). This landmark ruling narrowed the scope of blocked credits.

## Key Defense Points
1. Section 17(5) is an exhaustive list — if the item is not listed, ITC cannot be denied
2. The Safari Retreats judgment allows ITC on construction if the property is used for taxable outward supply
3. Motor vehicle ITC is allowed if used for further supply, transportation of passengers, or training
4. ITC reversal under Rule 42/43 must be recalculated annually — interim reversals are provisional
5. Free samples/gifts ITC denial applies only if no consideration is received
