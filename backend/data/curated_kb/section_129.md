# Section 129 — Detention, Seizure, and Release of Goods in Transit

## Key Ruling
Section 129 empowers a proper officer to **detain or seize goods in transit** if they are transported in contravention of CGST Act provisions (e.g., without e-way bill, without proper invoice).

## Process
1. Officer detains goods and conveyance
2. Issues **Form MOV-06** (notice of detention/seizure)
3. Owner/transporter can get release by:
   - **Option A:** Pay tax + 200% penalty (if owner comes forward)
   - **Option B:** Pay tax + 50% penalty + furnish security (if owner does not come forward)
4. If no payment within **14 days** → goods deemed to be confiscated under Section 130

## Key Defense Points
1. E-way bill validity — if e-way bill was valid at time of interception → detention is illegal
2. Minor discrepancies (e.g., vehicle number typo in e-way bill) → argue for release without penalty
3. Part B of e-way bill can be updated during transit → check if it was updatable
4. If goods are perishable → argue for immediate provisional release under Section 67(6)
5. Challenge if detention was by officer below rank of proper officer
6. If goods are not in transit (at warehouse/godown) → Section 129 does NOT apply
