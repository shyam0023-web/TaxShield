# Section 122 — Penalties for Specified Offences

## Key Ruling
Section 122 specifies penalties for **offences** under the CGST Act. Penalty can be imposed on any taxable person who commits specified offences including issuing fake invoices, availing fraudulent ITC, failing to issue invoices, etc.

## Penalty Structure
- **Section 122(1):** Penalty of ₹10,000 or **tax amount involved**, whichever is higher
- **Section 122(2):** Any registered person who helps in commission of offence → penalty up to ₹25,000
- **Section 122(3):** Any person who fails to appear when summoned → penalty up to ₹25,000

## Common Offences (Section 122(1))
1. Supply without invoice or with false invoice
2. Issue invoice without supply (fake/bogus invoices)
3. Collect tax but fail to remit within 3 months
4. Avail or utilize ITC without actual receipt of goods/services
5. Fraudulently obtain refund
6. Fail to obtain registration despite being liable
7. Furnish false information during registration
8. Obstruct/prevent officer from performing duties
9. Transport goods without prescribed documents
10. Suppress turnover leading to evasion of tax

## Key Defense Points
1. Penalty under 122(1) requires proving the offence — burden is on department
2. If offence is procedural (e.g., late filing) vs substantive (e.g., evasion) → penalty should be proportionate
3. Section 122 penalty is separate from demand under 73/74 — cannot be imposed as part of demand
4. First-time minor violations → argue for minimum penalty (₹10,000)
5. If offence relates to fake invoices → verify if investigation under Section 132 is more appropriate

## Related Provisions
- Section 125: General penalty (₹25,000 for unspecified offences)
- Section 130: Confiscation of goods/conveyances
- Section 132: Prosecution (criminal — for offences exceeding specified thresholds)
