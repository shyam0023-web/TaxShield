# Section 73 — Determination of Tax Not Paid (Non-Fraud Cases)

## Key Ruling
Section 73 applies to cases where tax was not paid, short-paid, erroneously refunded, or ITC was wrongly availed/utilized **without any allegation of fraud, willful misstatement, or suppression of facts**. Limitation period is **3 years** from the due date of filing annual return (GSTR-9) for the relevant financial year.

## Limitation Period
- **Time limit:** 3 years from due date of annual return
- **Due date of GSTR-9:** December 31st of the year following the financial year
- **Example:** FY 2017-18 → GSTR-9 due 31-Dec-2018 → SCN must be issued by **31-Dec-2021**

## COVID Extensions (CBIC Notifications)
- FY 2017-18: Extended to 31-Mar-2024 (Notification 09/2023)

## Pre-SCN Intimation (Section 73(5))
Before issuing an SCN under Section 73, the proper officer **must** issue a pre-show cause notice intimation in **Form DRC-01A**. Failure to issue this intimation is a **procedural defect**.

## Adjudication Timeline
- SCN must specify a time limit for reply (minimum 30 days)
- Adjudication order under 73(9) must be passed within **3 years** from the date of issuance of SCN

## Key Defense Points
1. If notice issued beyond 3-year limitation → **time-barred, liable to be quashed**
2. If pre-SCN intimation (DRC-01A) not issued → **procedural defect**
3. Section 73 cannot be invoked if department alleges fraud → must use Section 74
4. Demand computation must be detailed, not lump-sum
5. Personal hearing must be offered under Section 75(4)
