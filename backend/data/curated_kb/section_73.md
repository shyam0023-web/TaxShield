# Section 73 — Determination of Tax Not Paid (Non-Fraud Cases)

## Key Ruling
Section 73 applies to cases where tax was not paid, short-paid, erroneously refunded, or ITC was wrongly availed/utilized **without any allegation of fraud, willful misstatement, or suppression of facts**. Limitation period is **3 years** from the due date of filing annual return (GSTR-9) for the relevant financial year.

## Limitation Period
- **Time limit:** 3 years from due date of annual return
- **Due date of GSTR-9:** December 31st of the year following the financial year
- **Example:** FY 2017-18 → GSTR-9 due 31-Dec-2018 → SCN must be issued by **31-Dec-2021**

## COVID Extensions (CBIC Notifications)
- FY 2017-18: Extended to 31-Mar-2024 (Notification 09/2023)
- FY 2018-19: Extended to 31-Mar-2025 (Notification 09/2023)

## Pre-SCN Intimation (Section 73(5))
Before issuing an SCN under Section 73, the proper officer **must** issue a pre-show cause notice intimation in **Form DRC-01A**. Failure to issue this intimation is a **procedural defect**.

## Section 73(8) — Pay Within 30 Days to Avoid Penalty
If the taxpayer pays the full tax along with interest under Section 50 **within 30 days** of the SCN, no penalty is payable and all proceedings are deemed concluded.

## Section 73(9) — Penalty Provisions
If tax and interest are not paid within 30 days:
- Penalty = **10% of tax or Rs.10,000**, whichever is higher, under each Act (CGST/SGST separately)

## Adjudication Timeline
- SCN must specify a time limit for reply (minimum 30 days)
- Adjudication order under 73(9) must be passed within **3 years** from the date of issuance of SCN

## Key Defense Points
1. If notice issued beyond 3-year limitation → **time-barred, liable to be quashed**
2. If pre-SCN intimation (DRC-01A) not issued → **procedural defect**
3. Section 73 cannot be invoked if department alleges fraud → must use Section 74
4. Demand computation must be detailed, not lump-sum
5. Personal hearing must be offered under Section 75(4)
6. If taxpayer has already paid tax via DRC-03 → show proof, demand should be reduced accordingly
7. For GSTR-2A vs GSTR-3B (TDS) mismatch — verify if difference is due to timing of TDS credit vs return filing
8. For GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B mismatch — check if amendments in GSTR-1 were filed in subsequent periods

## Common SCN Patterns (Real-World)

### Pattern 1: TDS-GSTR-3B Turnover Difference
- **Discrepancy:** GSTR-2A (TDS invoice list) shows higher contract receipt than GSTR-3B declared turnover
- **Typical cause:** TDS credited by deductor in different periods than taxpayer's return filing
- **Defense:** Show that total turnover across periods matches; timing difference is not short declaration

### Pattern 2: GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B Turnover Difference
- **Discrepancy:** GSTR-1 outward supply exceeds GSTR-3B declared tax liability
- **Typical cause:** Credit notes, amendments, or nil-rated supplies in GSTR-1 not reflected in GSTR-3B
- **Defense:** Provide reconciliation showing GSTR-1 includes exempt/nil supplies, credit notes, or amendments

### Preceding Notice: ASMT-10 (Section 61 Scrutiny)
A Section 73 SCN is typically preceded by:
1. **Form ASMT-10** — scrutiny notice under Section 61 identifying discrepancies
2. Taxpayer replies in **Form ASMT-11** with explanations
3. If explanation not accepted → officer proceeds to Section 73 SCN

## Reply Format: Form GST ASMT-11 (Rule 99(2))

The standard reply to a Section 61 scrutiny notice (preceding Section 73 SCN) must be filed in **Form GST ASMT-11** with the following structure:

### Required Fields
1. **GSTIN** — Taxpayer's GSTIN
2. **Name** — Legal name of the taxpayer
3. **Detail of notice** — Reference No. and Date of the ASMT-10/SCN
4. **Tax Period** — Period covered (e.g., "April 2019 to March 2020")
5. **Reply to discrepancies** — Point-by-point response to each defect listed in the notice, with supporting evidence
6. **Amount Admitted and Paid, if Any** — Table with columns: Sr. No | Tax Period (From/To) | Act (CGST/SGST) | Tax (₹) | Interest (₹) | Others (₹) | Total (₹). Enter "-NA-" if nothing admitted.
7. **Verification** — Solemn declaration: "I hereby solemnly affirm and declare that the information given hereinabove is true and correct to the best of my/our knowledge and belief and nothing has been concealed therefrom."
8. **Signature** — Authorized Signatory with Name, Designation/Status, and Date

### Reply Strategy for Section 73(1) SCN
1. **Address each defect separately** with specific facts and figures
2. **Attach evidence:** DRC-03 challans for payments already made, GSTR-1/3B reconciliation, bank statements, invoices
3. **Highlight partial compliance:** If tax already paid for any defect, clearly show the payment proof to reduce demand
4. **Request reduction:** If interest is the only outstanding item, request computation from exact date of tax payment (not from original due date)
5. **Invoke Section 73(8):** If willing to pay, pay within 30 days to avoid penalty
6. **Challenge time-bar:** Check if SCN was issued within the 3-year limitation (including any COVID extensions)

## Relevant Provisions Cross-Referenced
