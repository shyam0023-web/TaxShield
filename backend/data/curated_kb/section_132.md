# Section 132 — Punishment for Certain Offences

## Summary
Section 132 prescribes criminal penalties for serious GST offences including fake invoicing, tax evasion beyond specified thresholds, and obstruction of officers.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 132(1):** Offences punishable with imprisonment:
  - (a) Supply without invoice or with false invoice
  - (b) Issues invoice without supply (fake invoicing)
  - (c) Collects tax but fails to pay to Government beyond 3 months
  - (d) Collects tax in contravention of provisions
  - (e) Fraudulently obtains refund
  - (f) Falsifies financial records
  - (g) Obstructs or prevents any officer in discharge of duties
  - (h) Tampers with evidence
  - (i) Disposes of/ tampers with goods that have been detained/seized/attached
- **Section 132(1)(i):** Tax evasion exceeding Rs. 5 crores — imprisonment up to 5 years + fine.
- **Section 132(1)(ii):** Tax evasion between Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 5 crore — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine.
- **Section 132(1)(iii):** Tax evasion between Rs. 1 crore to Rs. 2 crore — imprisonment up to 1 year + fine.
- **Section 132(4):** Offences under clauses (a) to (f) and (l) are cognizable and non-bailable if tax amount exceeds Rs. 5 crores.
- **Section 132(5):** All other offences are non-cognizable and bailable.
- **Section 132(6):** Previous sanction of Commissioner required before filing prosecution.

## Key Ruling
In **Jayachandran Alloys Pvt. Ltd v. Superintendent of GST**, the Supreme Court held that arrest under GST can only be made after the Commissioner has "reasons to believe" based on credible material, not mere suspicion. The power of arrest must be exercised with caution and cannot be routine.

## Key Defense Points
1. Commissioner's previous sanction under Section 132(6) is mandatory — prosecution without it is void
2. Arrest requires "reasons to believe" not "reasons to suspect" — mere suspicion is insufficient
3. For amounts below Rs. 5 crores, the offence is bailable and non-cognizable
4. Section 132 applies only for wilful and deliberate offences — genuine errors are not criminal
5. Compounding under Section 138 is available for most offences (except repeat offenders)
6. Anticipatory bail is maintainable in GST cases as held by multiple High Courts
7. The threshold for cognizable offence was raised from Rs. 2 crore to Rs. 5 crore by Finance Act 2020
