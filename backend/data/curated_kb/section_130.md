# Section 130 — Confiscation of Goods or Conveyances

## Key Ruling
Section 130 provides for **confiscation** of goods or conveyances used in commission of specified offences. This is the most severe penalty short of criminal prosecution.

## When Confiscation Applies
1. Goods supplied without invoice with intent to evade tax
2. Goods not accounted for (found excess in stock during search)
3. Goods supplied in violation of provisions leading to tax evasion
4. Goods detained under Section 129 and not released within prescribed time

## Process
1. Show cause notice specifying goods proposed for confiscation
2. Opportunity of hearing to owner/transporter
3. Adjudicating authority passes order of confiscation
4. Owner can redeem goods on payment of **fine in lieu of confiscation** (Section 130(2))

## Key Defense Points
1. Confiscation requires proof of **intent to evade** — mens rea must be established
2. Option to pay fine in lieu of confiscation — amount should be reasonable
3. Conveyance cannot be confiscated if transporter proves no knowledge of evasion
4. If goods are not liable for confiscation → entire penalty proceedings collapse
5. Challenge if personal hearing not given before confiscation order

## Related Provisions
- Section 129: Detention/seizure (precursor to confiscation)
- Section 122: Penalty (separate track)
- Section 132: Prosecution (criminal track, for amounts exceeding ₹5 crore)
