# Section 29 — Cancellation of GST Registration

## Summary
Section 29 deals with the cancellation of GST registration by the proper officer or voluntarily by the registered person. This is one of the most common notice types received by taxpayers.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 29(1):** Proper officer may cancel registration on specified grounds.
- **Section 29(2):** Grounds for cancellation:
  - (a) Contravention of Act provisions
  - (b) Non-filing of returns for continuous period (6 months for regular, 3 consecutive quarters for composition)
  - (c) Voluntary cancellation requested by person
  - (d) Registration obtained by fraud, willful misstatement or suppression of facts
  - (e) Death of proprietor (in case of proprietorship)
- **Section 29(3):** Cancellation of voluntary registration — only after expiry of one year from effective date.
- **Section 29(4):** Cancellation is without prejudice to liability for tax and penalties.
- **Section 29(5):** Payment of ITC reversal and tax on stock on date of cancellation.

## Key Ruling
In **Tvl. Suguna Cutpiece Center v. Appellate Deputy Commissioner**, the Madras High Court held that cancellation of registration for non-filing of returns is disproportionate if the taxpayer has subsequently filed all pending returns and paid dues. The proper officer must consider less drastic measures.

## Key Defense Points
1. Non-filing of returns alone is not sufficient for cancellation — taxpayer must be given opportunity to comply
2. Show Cause Notice under Rule 22 must specify the exact grounds for proposed cancellation
3. If returns are filed before the cancellation order, cancellation should not proceed
4. Section 30 provides for revocation within 30 days (extendable to 180 days per CBIC circular)
5. Cancellation ab initio (retrospective) requires strong justification — mere non-filing is insufficient
6. CBIC has issued multiple amnesty schemes allowing revival of cancelled registrations
