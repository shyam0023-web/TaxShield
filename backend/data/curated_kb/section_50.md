# Section 50 — Interest on Delayed Payment of Tax

## Summary
Section 50 imposes interest on delayed payment of tax. This is one of the most contested provisions as interest liability can sometimes exceed the tax demand itself.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 50(1):** Interest at 18% per annum on tax paid after the due date. Calculated from the day after the due date until the date of payment.
- **Section 50(2):** If ITC is wrongly availed and utilized, interest at 24% per annum.
- **Section 50(3):** Interest is calculated on net cash liability (after adjusting ITC available in electronic credit ledger). This amendment was made retrospectively w.e.f. 01.07.2017.

## Key Ruling
In **Megha Engineering & Infrastructures Ltd v. Commissioner of Central Tax**, the Supreme Court upheld that interest under Section 50(1) is payable on the net cash tax liability, NOT on gross liability. The retrospective amendment to Section 50 was held valid.

## Key Defense Points
1. Interest is on NET cash liability, not gross liability — ensure officer calculates correctly
2. The retrospective amendment to Section 50(3) w.e.f. 01.07.2017 benefits the taxpayer — rely on it
3. Interest at 24% applies ONLY when ITC is wrongly availed AND utilized — mere wrong availment without utilization attracts 18%
4. If tax was paid before issuance of SCN, interest should be calculated only until date of payment
5. Interest is compensatory in nature, not penal — cannot be imposed as punishment
6. Circular 192/04/2023 clarified that interest is automatic and mandatory, but must be calculated correctly
