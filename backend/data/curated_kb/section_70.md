# Section 70 — Power to Summon and Examine Persons

## Summary
Section 70 empowers the proper officer to summon any person to give evidence or produce documents during an inquiry or investigation under GST.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 70(1):** Proper officer may summon any person whose attendance is necessary for giving evidence or producing documents in any inquiry.
- **Section 70(2):** Every such inquiry shall be deemed to be a "judicial proceeding" within the meaning of the Indian Penal Code.

## Key Ruling
In **Canon India Pvt Ltd v. Commissioner of Customs**, the Supreme Court (though a customs case, applied to GST) held that summons must be issued by the jurisdictional officer and the person summoned has the right to know the nature of the inquiry.

## Key Defense Points
1. The person summoned has the right to legal representation during examination
2. Statements recorded under Section 70 are not admissible as evidence unless corroborated
3. Summons must specify the purpose — fishing inquiries are not permitted
4. The summoned person can request adjournment on valid grounds
5. Failure to comply can result in prosecution under Section 174 of IPC (now BNS), but arrest is not automatic
