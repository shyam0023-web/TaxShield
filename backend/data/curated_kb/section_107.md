# Section 107 — Appeals to Appellate Authority

## Summary
Section 107 provides the first appellate remedy under GST. Any person aggrieved by an adjudication order or decision can appeal to the Appellate Authority within 3 months.

## Key Provisions
- **Section 107(1):** Appeal to Appellate Authority within 3 months from the date of communication of order.
- **Section 107(2):** Officer of Central/State tax may also appeal within 6 months.
- **Section 107(4):** Condonation of delay — Appellate Authority may allow appeal filed within a further period of 1 month (total 4 months).
- **Section 107(6):** Mandatory pre-deposit:
  - **10% of disputed tax** (for the appellant)
  - Maximum pre-deposit capped at Rs. 25 crores for CGST and Rs. 25 crores for SGST
- **Section 107(7):** Stay of demand — balance demand stayed pending appeal once pre-deposit is made.
- **Section 107(11):** Appellate Authority must pass order within 1 year from the date of filing appeal.

## Key Ruling
In **Commissioner of CGST v. M/s Ravi Trading Company**, the Tribunal held that the 10% pre-deposit is mandatory and cannot be waived. However, the pre-deposit amount is to be calculated only on the disputed tax amount, not on penalty or interest.

## Key Defense Points
1. The 3-month limitation begins from the date of "communication" — actual receipt, not date of order
2. Pre-deposit of 10% of disputed tax is sufficient — full demand cannot be enforced during appeal
3. If the order lacks a DIN (Document Identification Number), it is void ab initio per Circular 128/2019
4. Appellate Authority has wide powers under Section 107(11) to confirm, modify, annul, or remand
5. If Appellate Authority fails to decide within 1 year, the appeal does not lapse — it remains pending
6. Recovery proceedings under Section 79 are automatically stayed once pre-deposit is made
